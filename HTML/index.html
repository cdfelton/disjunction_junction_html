In this study you'll be asked to consider two sentences and rate how likely they are to both be true at the same time. The key to keep in mind is that the experiment isn't asking you how likely either of the sentences are to be true themselves, but instead whether they are more or less likely to both be true together if one is true. You'll complete a few guided practice trials to get a feel for the task and then proceed to the real experiment.
During each trial you will be presented with 2 sentences and a slider that ranges from zero to one. You will move the slider to indicate how likely you think the situations described in the two sentences are to occur together (both be true). Rating a trial "0" indicates that it is impossible for the sentences to both be true, while rating a trial "1" indicates that if one is true the other MUST also be true. Putting the slider in the middle indicates that the situations described are equally likely to occur together or separately.
When you're satisfied with your rating, you can click the submit button to move on to the next item.
